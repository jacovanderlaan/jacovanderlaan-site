Flagship project

de Volksbank & ASN — where Model-Driven Data Engineering was built.

As Lead Modeller, I led the design of a new, model-driven data warehouse for de Volksbank's brands (ASN, SNS, RegioBank, BLG Wonen) — one of the most influential MDDE implementations in the Dutch data community, and the proving ground for the method I bring to other teams.

The idea

Separate what the data means (functional modeling) from how it runs (technical engineering); merge structure and movement into one metadata framework.

How it works

Rules and models defined as metadata (entity-first) — the platform generates database-agnostic transformations, quality tests and docs, consistently.

Why it mattered

Business could trust the data because physical designs are regenerated from logical models. Lineage and historization built in.

The payoff

Agility, independence from any single database tech, fewer errors, governance that's inherent — delivered in short cycles, not waterfall.

I invited CrossBreeze (Harmen Wessels & Willem Otten), whom I met on the Rabobank project, to help build the framework.

Signature engagement

Migrating legacy SQL — and proving it's identical.

My largest recent delivery: migrating complex legacy SQL to a governed, bi-temporal integration layer for a bank's Risk & Finance platform. The hard part isn't rewriting — it's proving the new pipeline produces exactly the same data. So I built the tooling to prove it.
